How It Works

When you open Cipher, you will be given several choices. They all have presets, so you don't have to do anything if you don't want to — just hit Run Scan. That is the box store hammer; it gets the job done, but it is plain and not balanced to fit your desires. Every filter you change balances the hammer to you.

Cipher runs on the CoinGecko Demo API and gives you three ways to scan: Trending surfaces pools gaining momentum right now. Top Volume returns the highest 24-hour volume pools on the selected chain. New Tokens hunts for the freshest pools within an age window you define — new launches, early movers, the highest-risk highest-reward bucket. You need a free CoinGecko API key to use it; that key lives in your browser and never leaves it.

So let's talk about those choices. You can select one of eight blockchains — Solana, Ethereum, Base, BSC, Arbitrum, Optimism, Polygon, Avalanche — or scan them all at once with the global All Chains mode. The scan takes a few seconds and returns up to 40 results, ranked by composite score so the strongest signals surface first. Tier One chains get the full GoPlus security treatment in Forge; Cipher keeps it clean and fast with on-chain data only.

The results table has a clickable link to GeckoTerminal for that particular token, which accesses the free version so you can do some research. It also has an address copy feature, so if you have a preferred charting tool, you can copy and paste into that.

The optional trade journal will be used to refine the filters and give others a chance to learn what signals to watch for. Neither I nor anyone else involved in this project will ever ask how much you invested; it is none of our business. The research is about what you traded and why — the minimum liquidity was X, the moving average was Y, you had a gut feeling, or you liked the name. The information may also be used to train an ElizaOS agent.
